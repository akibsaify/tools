How Age Is Calculated

Calculating age might seem straightforward, but it involves careful handling of varying month lengths and leap years. Here is how this calculator determines your exact age:

The calculator finds the difference between your date of birth and the target date by computing the gap in years, months, and days separately. It starts by comparing the day of the month. If the target day is smaller than the birth day, it borrows days from the previous month (using that month’s actual length). Then it checks months — if the target month is smaller than the birth month (after any borrowing), it borrows 12 months from the year count.

This approach ensures accurate results even for edge cases like people born on 29 February (leap day) or at the end of months with 30 or 31 days. The total days calculation uses the actual calendar difference, which correctly accounts for all leap years in between.

For additional statistics, the calculator computes:

  • Total months = (years × 12) + remaining months
  • Total weeks = total days ÷ 7 (rounded down)
  • Total days = exact day count between the two dates
  • Next birthday = days remaining until your next birthday from the target date

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my exact age?

Enter your date of birth in the first field and the date you want to calculate your age as on in the second field (it defaults to today). The calculator will instantly show your age in years, months, and days, along with total months, total weeks, and total days you have lived. It handles leap years, varying month lengths, and edge cases like being born on the 29th, 30th, or 31st of a month.

What is the age limit for government jobs in India?

The general age limit for central government jobs in India is 18 to 35 years for the general (unreserved) category. OBC candidates get a relaxation of 3 years (up to 38 years), SC/ST candidates get 5 years (up to 40 years), and Persons with Disabilities (PwD) get 10 years (up to 45 years). Some specific posts and state governments may have different age limits, so always check the official notification.

What is the minimum age for voter ID in India?

You must be at least 18 years old on the qualifying date (1st January of the year of revision of the electoral roll) to be eligible for a voter ID in India. You can apply online through the National Voters’ Service Portal (NVSP) or by submitting Form 6 at your nearest Electoral Registration Office. Use this age calculator to check if you will be 18 by the qualifying date.

What is the minimum age for a driving licence in India?

In India, you can obtain a driving licence for motorcycles without gear (up to 50cc) at the age of 16 with the consent of a guardian. For motorcycles with gear and cars (non-transport vehicles), the minimum age is 18 years. For transport or commercial vehicles, you must be at least 20 years old. A learner’s licence can be applied for at the same minimum ages.

What is the retirement age in India?

The retirement age for central government employees in India is 60 years. For state government employees, it varies between 58 and 62 years depending on the state. Judges of the Supreme Court retire at 65, and High Court judges retire at 62. In the private sector, the retirement age is typically 58 to 60 years, as decided by the company’s policy. Use this calculator to find how many years you have until retirement.

At what age do senior citizen benefits start in India?

Under the Income Tax Act, senior citizen benefits begin at age 60. Senior citizens (aged 60 to 79) enjoy a higher basic exemption limit of Rs 3 lakh compared to Rs 2.5 lakh for those below 60. Super senior citizens (aged 80 and above) get an even higher exemption of Rs 5 lakh. Additionally, senior citizens receive higher interest rates on fixed deposits from most banks, priority service at banks and hospitals, and concessions on rail travel.
